Kevin Yu, PhD, is a Research Associate in the Department of Radiology and Biomedical Imaging at Yale School of Medicine. His research focuses on multimodal neuroimaging, including magnetoencephalography (MEG) and MRI, to study brain function and its relationship to underlying neurobiological systems.

A neuroscientist by training, Kevin's work centers on how concussive and subconcussive head impacts alter brain function, with an emphasis on aperiodic neural activity and excitation and inhibition balance. He integrates multimodal datasets across MEG, MRI, and behavioral and clinical measures to identify neurophysiological biomarkers of brain injury. His broader interests include translational neuroscience and clinical research, neurotechnology and brain and computer interfaces, computational modeling and signal analysis, and industry applications of neuroimaging and digital health. Kevin has contributed to peer-reviewed publications and presented his work at conferences including RSNA and BIOMAG.
