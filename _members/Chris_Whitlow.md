Christopher T. Whitlow, MD, PhD, MHA, FASFNR, is a physician-scientist at Yale School of Medicine with cross-disciplinary appointments in the Departments of Radiology and Biomedical Engineering. He serves as Professor and Chair of Radiology and Biomedical Imaging and Assistant Dean for Translational Research, as well as Radiologist-in-Chief across the Yale New Haven Health System, overseeing clinical imaging operations in support of the tripartite mission of Yale School of Medicine and Yale New Haven Health.

Dr. Whitlow is the founder and director of the Radiology Informatics and Image Processing Laboratory (RIIPL), which leverages multi-site big data from medical imaging for advanced high-throughput signal processing, analysis, and rendering, including 3D printing, augmented reality, and virtual reality of multimodal imaging data. As an imaging data scientist with a strong computer science background, he applies a range of computational techniques to research and clinical imaging data, including graph-theoretical network methods and artificial intelligence and deep learning.

He has secured more than 23 million dollars in research funding as lead or principal investigator from the NIH and private foundations, and more than 55 million dollars in team-science co-investigator funding. His work has resulted in more than 400 peer-reviewed journal articles, abstracts, and book chapters, and is frequently cited in the literature. At the national level, Dr. Whitlow has been elected to the executive leadership of the American Society of Functional Neuroradiology, serving as its President in 2023. Through his research, clinical practice, and national and international service, he works at the cutting edge of clinical radiology, imaging data science, and translational research, developing and deploying new imaging technology from bench to patient care.
