Jeremy Hudson joined RIIPL at the Yale School of Medicine in November 2025, bringing nearly five years of prior experience as a Programmer/Analyst on a radiology research team. His work focuses on medical imaging pipelines for radiology research, including MRI and PET analysis, ADNI longitudinal processing, and multi-site harmonization.

He also builds deep learning systems for classification, segmentation, and disease prediction, running them on dedicated infrastructure. Much of his work involves batch-processing large public imaging collections such as ADNI and ABIDE, streamlining the pipeline work that supports the team's research. Jeremy holds a B.S. in Computer Science from UNC Greensboro. Outside of work, he keeps up with ML research, science fiction, fitness, and gaming.
